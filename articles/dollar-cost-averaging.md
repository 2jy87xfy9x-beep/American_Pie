## What It Is

Dollar-cost averaging buys a fixed dollar amount of an asset on a schedule regardless of price — more shares when cheap, fewer when expensive.

It is not magic alpha — it is emotional prosthetic + slight convexity in volatile paths versus lump-sum in some historical studies — but in crisis psychology it saved people from all-or-nothing timing.

## The Math

**$300**/month into a volatile fund: if month A price high → few shares; month B crash → many shares. Weighted average purchase price trends below simple average of the two prints — illustration only, not promise.

If you instead hoard **$3,600** for a year then lump-sum after fear fades, opportunity cost of idle **{{your_cash}}** during low **{{high_yield_savings_rate}}** environments differs — DCA trades some expected return for sleep.

Against **{{your_monthly_expenses}}**, a disciplined **5%** auto-transfer is **0.05 × {{your_monthly_income}}** when income stable — disappears if layoffs hit unless you cancel fast.
