## What It Is

COBRA lets you keep your former employer’s group health plan for limited months by paying the full premium — employer share plus your share plus a small admin fee — yourself.

Sticker shock is the feature. Continuity of care is the trade you buy with cash.

## The Math

If employer-subsidized coverage cost you $120/month out of pocket but the COBRA notice quotes $1,600/month family premium, the incremental burn is **$1,480/month**.

Stack that on **{{your_monthly_expenses}}** without adjusting anything else and the monthly hole versus **{{your_monthly_income}}** (now maybe zero) widens by exactly that premium until you switch plans or income returns.

If UI pays roughly half of old wages, **{{income_gap}}** already existed — COBRA can consume most of what UI pays, which is why Medicaid, marketplace plans, or spouses’ coverage enter the math fast.
