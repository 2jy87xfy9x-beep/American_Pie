## What It Is

Diversification spreads bets so no single failure erases **{{your_net_worth}}** — different assets, geographies, currencies, durations.

Crisis correlation spikes (“everything sold”) remind diversification is about survival, not eliminating pain.

## Your Number

Concentration in **{{your_investment_value}}** matters — one employer stock + S&P-heavy 401k + rental same city = false diversity.

**{{your_cash}}** is diversification versus illiquidity.

## The Math

If portfolio **60/40** stocks/bonds,stocks drop **−30%**, bonds **+5%** illustration → blended ≈ **0.6×(−30) + 0.4×5 = −16%** — still hurts, Less than **−30%**.

Check largest holding ÷ **{{your_investment_value}}** — **>15%** single name demands thesis note.

## What People Did

**What most people did**

They bought ten large-cap funds with overlapping top holdings — called it variety.

**What financially stable people did**

They used correlation matrices lightly — at least avoided duplicate factor tilts.

▸ If you had known this before 2008

You’d cringe at “diversified” portfolio **0.92** correlated to S&P — specific number from old statement footnote you ignored.
