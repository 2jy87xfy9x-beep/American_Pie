## What It Is

Bear Stearns was an investment bank — heavy in mortgage-backed bonds and short-term funding. When counterparties stopped trusting its balance sheet in March 2008, the firm died in a weekend and was handed to JP Morgan with Federal Reserve assistance.

You did not need a brokerage account to feel it. Credit tightened for ordinary borrowers the same week dinner-table conversation learned the phrase “liquidity crisis.”

## What People Did

**What most people did**

They refreshed CNBC for drama, assumed rescue meant “fine,” and ignored that their HELOC margin could reset anyway.

**What financially stable people did**

They pulled a one-page balance sheet: cash, brokerage, employer stock concentrations, next twelve months of required cash flows. If anything sat in a single firm’s stock or funds without FDIC limits, they acknowledged headline risk explicitly.
