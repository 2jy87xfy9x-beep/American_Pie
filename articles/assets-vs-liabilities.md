## What It Is

Assets are things with value you own — cash, brokerage, home equity hopefully. Liabilities are obligations — cards, student loans, cosigned nonsense.

Confusing consumer durability with assets is how **{{your_monthly_expenses}}** wins silently.

## Your Number

**{{your_cash}}** is liquid asset star.

**{{your_credit_card_debt}}** is liability costing **APR** monthly.

**{{your_credit_score}}** signals how cheaply your liabilities refinance.

## The Math

If **{{your_credit_card_debt}}** = **$6,500** at **22%** APR, interest ≈ **$6,500 × 0.22 ÷ 12 ≈ $119**/month before principal — compare to **{{your_monthly_expenses}}** slices you “cannot cut.”

Paydown **$500** principal → new balance **$6,000** → interest next month drops — snowball isn’t vibes; it’s algebra.

**{{your_investment_value}}** volatility is asset risk; **{{your_monthly_rent}}** obligation is liability-like even if not on classical balance sheet.
