FEATURED ARTICLE
What This Archive Is and Why It Begins in January 2008

This archive covers the period January 2008 to the present. It contains articles written in the voices of institutional documents, wire reports, financial press analysis, local newspapers, and long-form features. None of the articles announce what they are teaching. The teaching happens in the gap between what an article describes and what the reader quietly calculates about themselves while reading it.

The archive opens in January 2008 because that month carries a specific quality: everything appears stable. The ticker at the bottom of this page is barely moving. The featured article this month is the kind of thing a careful person saves because it seems important but not urgent. By September of this year both of those facts will have changed entirely.

In this prototype the articles you encounter describe the simulation itself — what each era does, how the ticker behaves, what the vehicles represent. In the working simulation those same article slots contain journalism. The format is identical. The subject is different.

American Pie Wire · January 2008

This is the newspaper edition of the American Pie archive. The same years, the same vehicles, the same emotional arc as the encyclopedia edition — rendered as a broadsheet rather than a reference document. The masthead is the theme toggle. The shipping notices at the bottom are the ticker.

The archive is currently in Era 1 of 9. The emotional temperature of January 2008 is calm. The ticker is in its synchronized state — it changes only when you navigate. The left column carries five vehicle notices and a blank sixth notice awaiting your situation.

In this prototype each article slot contains text that describes what the working article in that slot will do. The voice is the same. The format is the same. The subject, in the working simulation, is not the simulation itself.

The archive emblem in the encyclopedia edition responds to audio state. In the newspaper edition the audio feature occupies the box in the left column labeled Tonight's Programme. In this prototype the audio system is not wired. The record graphic does not spin. In the working simulation it will.
